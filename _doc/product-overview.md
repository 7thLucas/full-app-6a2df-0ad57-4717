# Product Overview — BrewBooks *(working title)*

## What it is

BrewBooks is a Point of Sale (POS) and financial management application built specifically for coffee shops. It combines the everyday transaction workflow of a modern POS with the accounting depth of QuickBooks — in a single unified platform, with no manual sync between systems.

Every sale at the counter auto-books to the financial ledger. End-of-day reconciliation is automated. Monthly reporting is self-generated. Owners run their entire business from one screen instead of three.

## Target users

| Persona | Role | Primary need |
|---|---|---|
| **Owner** | Business decision-maker | P&L visibility, tax readiness, financial health at a glance |
| **Barista / counter staff** | Daily POS user | Fast, intuitive order entry and payment processing |
| **Manager** | Operations oversight | Shift management, daily summaries, inventory alerts |

## Core positioning

Most coffee shops stitch together one tool to take orders and a separate one to manage money — then spend 30–60 minutes every evening reconciling between them. BrewBooks closes that gap: the transaction and the bookkeeping entry are the same event.

**Category**: Coffee-shop POS + integrated financial management  
**Differentiator**: Unified ledger — every POS transaction writes directly to the financial record with no export, no CSV, no sync step  
**Competitive anchors**: Square / Toast (POS), QuickBooks / Wave (accounting)

## Key capabilities

### POS & Ordering
- Menu management with drink/food categories and modifiers (size, milk type, add-ons)
- Order entry, payment processing (card + cash), tip collection
- Receipt generation (digital + print)
- Open tab management

### Sales & Reporting
- Real-time sales dashboard (today / this week / this month)
- Breakdown by item, category, shift, or time of day
- Comparison views (week-over-week, month-over-month)

### Financial Management
- Automatic revenue booking from every POS transaction
- Expense logging and categorization
- Profit & loss statements (live, not week-delayed)
- Sales tax tracking and accountant-ready export
- End-of-day cash drawer reconciliation (automated)

### Inventory
- Ingredient-level inventory tracking (coffee beans, syrups, milk)
- Low-stock alerts triggered before you run out mid-rush
- Usage estimation from sales volume

## Brand & tone

**Voice**: Practical, direct, built for the rhythm of a coffee shop. Not enterprise-bloated. An interface a barista can navigate mid-rush and an owner can trust for month-end.  
**Aesthetic**: Clean, warm, modern — espresso-brown primary, cream accents, uncluttered typography.  
**Persona feel**: QuickBooks reliability meets Square simplicity, with coffee-shop DNA throughout.

## Strategic principles

1. **Coffee-shop-first** — Menu structures, modifiers, and inventory units are native to café operations, not generic retail.
2. **Unified ledger** — POS and accounting are the same system. No export step, no reconciliation gap.
3. **Operator-speed UI** — Every key action (ringing an order, running a report) reachable in ≤ 3 taps.
4. **MVP scope** — Single-location coffee shop. Multi-location is a roadmap item.

## North Star metric

**Verified operation**: Sale completed at POS (order entered + payment processed + revenue auto-booked to ledger)  
**Target volume**: 1,200–1,800 sales/week (200–300 orders/day × 6 operating days — adjust to shop size)  
**Value driver**: Elimination of manual reconciliation overhead — ~45 min/day → ~5 min/day, recovering ~4 hrs/week of owner or manager time
